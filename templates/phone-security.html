Smartphone Security

Reduce risk from malicious apps, account takeovers, and unsafe networks. Use the checklist to build a strong, repeatable defense.

apps Malicious Apps

Identify fake apps by checking developer reputation and reviews. Be cautious with apps requesting unnecessary permissions (camera + contacts, SMS + microphone, etc.).

sim_card SIM Swapping

SIM swapping lets an attacker receive SMS verification codes by moving your number to their SIM. Prevent it with stronger 2FA and account protections.

wifi_lock Public Wi‑Fi Risks

Public Wi‑Fi can expose you to interception and fake hotspots. Prefer mobile data, avoid sensitive logins, and use a VPN.

security How to check if your phone is compromised
expand_more
  • Look for battery drain, unexpected data usage, and new admin/Accessibility permissions.
  • Check for unknown device administrators and remove them immediately.
  • Review notifications for suspicious "security alerts" from random apps.
  • Reinstall core apps from official stores after a suspected compromise.
rule Best practices for app permissions
expand_more
  • Prefer "Allow only while using the app" for location/microphone.
  • Reject SMS permissions for apps that don't need messaging.
  • Use your OS privacy dashboards to spot permission-heavy apps.
  • Revoke permissions after you finish tasks that require them.
devices How to set up remote wipe
expand_more
  • Enable "Find My" (iOS) or "Find My Device" (Android) inside your account settings.
  • Verify the correct recovery email/phone is linked to the device.
  • Turn on location permissions for the tracking service.
  • Practice the remote wipe flow once so you're ready during an emergency.
