Data Protection

Safeguard your personal information from misuse and theft

Why Data Protection Matters

Your personal data is valuable - to criminals, marketers, and data brokers. Personal information can be used for identity theft, fraud, targeted scams, or sold to third parties. Once data is leaked, you can't undo it. Protecting your data prevents misuse and keeps you safe.

Data breaches affect millions of people annually. Companies like Target, Equifax, and Facebook have exposed hundreds of millions of records. You need to assume your data may be compromised and take protective measures.

Key Security Practices

1

Use Encryption Tools

Encrypt sensitive files and communications. Use VeraCrypt for files, Signal for messages, or ProtonMail for email. Encryption ensures that even if data is stolen, it remains unreadable without the encryption key.

✅ Tools: VeraCrypt, Signal, ProtonMail
❌ Avoid: Storing sensitive files unencrypted
2

Limit Personal Info Shared

Only provide necessary information when required. Question why data is needed. Many websites ask for unnecessary personal details. Declining to share reduces your exposure to data breaches.

✅ Do: Ask "Why do you need this?"
❌ Don't: Blindly fill out all form fields
3

Review Privacy Settings

Regularly check privacy settings on social media, email, and online services. Minimize data sharing and make accounts private. Websites often have default settings that share more than you want.

✅ Do: Monthly privacy setting reviews
❌ Don't: Leave default privacy settings
4

Don't Overshare on Social Media

Avoid posting sensitive information (address, phone, birthdate, vacation dates, workplace). Criminals use this information for targeted attacks, identity theft, or physical targeting (burglary while you're away).

❌ Don't post: Full birthdate, address, phone number
✅ Do post: Only non-sensitive updates
5

Use VPN on Public Networks

Always use a VPN (Virtual Private Network) when connecting to public WiFi. VPNs encrypt all your traffic, preventing hackers on the same network from seeing your data, passwords, or activity.

✅ Use: NordVPN, ExpressVPN, Proton VPN
❌ Don't: Access sensitive data on unsecured WiFi
6

Monitor Your Accounts

Regularly check bank statements, credit card transactions, and credit reports for suspicious activity. The earlier you detect fraud, the easier it is to stop. Check credit reports annually (free at annualcreditreport.com).

✅ Do: Check accounts weekly/monthly
❌ Don't: Ignore unknown transactions
7

Use Privacy-Focused Services

Choose services that respect privacy. Use DuckDuckGo instead of Google, ProtonMail instead of Gmail, Signal instead of WhatsApp, Firefox instead of Chrome. These services don't track or sell your data.

✅ Privacy-focused: DuckDuckGo, ProtonMail, Signal
❌ Data collectors: Google, Facebook, WhatsApp
8

Secure Your Home Network

Change default WiFi passwords, enable WPA3 (or WPA2) encryption, hide your SSID, disable WPS, and update your router firmware. A weak home network puts all connected devices at risk.

✅ Do: Use strong WiFi password + WPA3
❌ Don't: Use default router password

Understanding Data Breaches

A data breach occurs when attackers access a company's database containing your personal information. This can happen through hacking, insider theft, or negligent security practices. When a breach occurs, your data may be:

  • Used directly for identity theft or fraud
  • Sold on the dark web to other criminals
  • Published publicly to embarrass or harm you
  • Held for ransom by ransomware attackers
  • Used for targeted phishing or scam attacks

Types of Personal Data to Protect

Highly Sensitive (🔴)

Social Security Number, Passport Number, Financial account credentials, Medical records, Government IDs

Sensitive (🟠)

Full name, Address, Phone number, Email address, Date of birth, Credit card information

Moderately Sensitive (🟡)

Employment history, Education records, Online usernames, IP addresses, Browsing history

Less Sensitive (🟢)

Publicly shared information, Generic profile details, Non-identifying preferences

Privacy Rights and Resources

  • GDPR (Europe): Gives you right to know, access, delete, and port your data
  • CCPA (California): Gives Californians privacy rights similar to GDPR
  • PIPEDA (Canada): Protects personal information collection and use
  • Your Rights: You can request companies delete your data (with limitations)
  • Data Subject Requests: Legally request copies of your data from companies
  • Opt-out Options: Opt out of data sales and targeted advertising

Free Tools and Resources

  • haveibeenpwned.com: Check if your email was in data breaches
  • annualcreditreport.com: Free annual credit reports (all 3 bureaus)
  • firefox.com/privacy/products: Firefox privacy tools
  • privacytools.io: Directory of privacy-focused software
  • 1password.com/passwordgenerator: Secure password generator
  • Signal: Encrypted messaging app
  • ProtonMail: Encrypted email service (free version available)
  • Tor Browser: Anonymous web browsing

Data Protection Checklist

  • ☐ Review social media privacy settings monthly
  • ☐ Check financial accounts for unauthorized activity
  • ☐ Pull credit reports annually (all 3 bureaus)
  • ☐ Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts
  • ☐ Enable two-factor authentication where available
  • ☐ Check "Have I Been Pwned" for email breaches
  • ☐ Use a VPN on public WiFi
  • ☐ Encrypt sensitive files
  • ☐ Consider identity theft protection services
  • ☐ Shred sensitive documents before discarding
  • ☐ Opt out of data broker lists
  • ☐ Review app permissions on phone/computer

If Your Data is Compromised

  • Act quickly - time is critical in identity theft cases
  • Change passwords on all affected accounts immediately
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts
  • Contact companies affected and request account freeze/lock
  • Place fraud alert with credit bureaus (free)
  • Consider credit freeze (prevents new accounts opened in your name)
  • Monitor credit reports closely for next 2-3 years
  • Report to FTC at identitytheft.gov
  • Document everything and keep records
  • Consider identity theft protection service
  • File police report if fraud occurred
  • Contact your insurance company about coverage
